Robotic Arm

Introduction

The aim of this project is to produce a flexible and user-friendly mechanical arm that can reliably pick up any object with a reasonable weight.


Important Components

An effective robotic arm needs at least a shoulder, elbow, and hand just like a typical human arm. However, to ensure that the robotic arm is flexible, an additional pivot before the shoulder is necessary to give the arm 360° rotational control.



Base Support

As the robotic arm extends itself forward, the amount of torque applied on the arm as a whole dramatically increases and a greater support force is necessary to counteract that torque. If not, the robotic arm will surely fall over and cause significant damage to itself. A simple solution would be to increase the area and weight of the base support. However, there must be a balance between the maximum arm length and the base support's area and weight.



Pivot

The pivot allows rotation about the y-axis(pointing towards the sky).



Shoulder

The shoulder provides rotation about the x-axis (pointing along the arm).



Elbow

The elbow design is essential because it allows the arm to pick up objects that are within or beyond the reach of the shoulder.



Claw

The claw must be designed such that adequate grip and grip strength is sufficient to pick up a wide range of items. *NOTE: Rubber or rough material are good choices to wrap around the claw fingers.

Pivot

The pivot's main task is to provide the robotic arm 360° rotation about the y-axis. The pivot must not be too large that carrying the robotic arm around is cumbersome but not too small that the amount of force required to rotate the robotic arm is unattainable for a NEMA-17 stepper motor.
